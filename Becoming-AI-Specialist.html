1

🌱 Foundation Phase (Months 1-3)

⏱️ 3 months | Build your mathematical and programming foundation
  • Complete Andrew Ng's Machine Learning Course (Coursera)
    💡 Take notes, do ALL assignments, aim for 80%+ quiz scores. Budget 6-8 hours/week for 11 weeks.
  • Master Python programming (complete Python Crash Course book)
    💡 Code along with every example. Build the 3 projects: Alien Invasion game, data visualization, web app.
  • Learn NumPy, Pandas, and Matplotlib libraries
    💡 Practice with real datasets from Kaggle. Create 5 different chart types with Matplotlib.
  • Refresh linear algebra (3Blue1Brown Essence of Linear Algebra)
    💡 Watch all 15 videos, take notes on key concepts. Understand: dot products, eigenvalues, transformations.
  • Study probability and statistics fundamentals
    💡 Focus on: normal distribution, Bayes' theorem, p-values. Use Khan Academy Statistics.
  • Complete first ML project: predict house prices using scikit-learn
    💡 Use Kaggle's House Prices dataset. Try Linear Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost.
  • Set up your development environment (Python, Jupyter, Git)
    💡 Install Anaconda. Learn basic Git commands: add, commit, push.
  • Create GitHub portfolio and document your first project
    💡 Create README.md with: project description, installation steps, usage examples, results.
2

🤖 Modern AI: LLMs, Agents & Prompt Engineering (Months 4-8)

⏱️ 5 months | Master the current AI revolution - this is where the money is RIGHT NOW
  • Master prompt engineering fundamentals and advanced techniques 💼
    💡 Learn: few-shot prompting, chain-of-thought, role prompting, system prompts. Practice on ChatGPT and Claude.
  • Advanced prompt architecture and context engineering 💼
    💡 Master: context window optimization, information hierarchy, attention patterns, context compression.
  • Learn to work with OpenAI GPT APIs
    💡 Build: text summarizer, Q&A bot, content generator. Learn about tokens, temperature, max_tokens.
  • Build RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems with LangChain 💼
    💡 Start simple: PDF Q&A bot. Steps: document loading → text splitting → embeddings → vector store → retrieval → generation.
  • Create AI agents using frameworks like CrewAI or AutoGen 💼
    💡 Build a multi-agent research team: one agent searches web, another analyzes, third writes summary.
  • Learn vector databases (Pinecone, Weaviate, ChromaDB)
    💡 Understand embeddings first. Build semantic search over your own text corpus. ChromaDB is easiest to start.
  • Build AI SaaS product prototype (this will get you clients!) 💼
    💡 Ideas: PDF chat tool, content optimizer, research assistant. Include: user auth, payment (Stripe), usage limits.
3

💼 Specialization & Research (Months 9-14)

⏱️ 6 months | Choose your specialty and start contributing to research
  • Choose your specialization (LLMs/Agents, Computer Vision, Robotics, AI Safety, etc.)
    💡 Research market demand, salary ranges, and your interests. Focus on one area to become the go-to expert.
  • Read 50+ recent papers in your chosen field (use arXiv)
    💡 Start with survey papers, then dive into specific techniques. Take notes and implement key algorithms.
  • Implement 5+ state-of-the-art models from scratch
    💡 Don't just use libraries - understand the math and code it yourself. This separates experts from users.
  • Contribute to open-source AI projects (GitHub AI projects)
    💡 Fix bugs, add features, improve documentation. Build your reputation in the community.
  • Start a technical blog and write 10+ posts (Medium, Dev.to)
    💡 Explain complex concepts simply. Share your learning journey. This builds your personal brand.
  • Launch your first AI consulting project ($5K+ revenue)
    💡 Start with local businesses. Offer prompt engineering, RAG systems, or AI strategy consulting.
4

🔬 Expert & Thought Leader (Months 15-18)

⏱️ 4+ months | Establish yourself as a recognized expert
  • Publish your first research paper
    💡 Submit to conferences like NeurIPS, ICML, or ICLR. Even workshop papers count and build credibility.
  • Speak at major AI conferences
    💡 Start with local meetups, then regional conferences. Share your expertise and unique insights.
  • Build 50K+ following on professional platforms
    💡 Be consistent on LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube. Share valuable content daily, engage with community.
  • Launch your own AI course or workshop
    💡 Teach your specialty on Udemy, Coursera, or independently. This creates passive income streams.
  • Build relationships with top researchers and companies
    💡 Attend conferences, join AI labs, collaborate on projects. Your network determines your net worth.
